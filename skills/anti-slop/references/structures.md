# Structural Patterns to Avoid

These patterns are language-agnostic. They appear in both Russian and English AI text.

## Binary Contrasts

False drama through negation-then-assertion. State Y directly.

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| "Not because X. Because Y." | Telegraphed reversal |
| "[X] isn't the problem. [Y] is." | Formulaic reframe |
| "The answer isn't X. It's Y." | Predictable pivot |
| "It feels like X. It's actually Y." | Setup/reveal cliche |
| "not just X but also Y" | Additive hedge |

**RU equivalents:** "Не потому что X. А потому что Y.", "Проблема не в X. Проблема в Y.", "Не просто X, но и Y."

**Fix:** State Y directly. Drop the negation.

## Negative Listing

Listing what something is NOT before revealing what it IS.

"Not a X. Not a Y. A Z." — The reader doesn't need the runway. State Z.

**RU:** "Это не X. Это не Y. Это Z."

## Dramatic Fragmentation

Sentence fragments for false profundity.

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| "[Noun]. That's it. That's the [thing]." | Performative simplicity |
| "X. And Y. And Z." | Staccato drama |

**RU:** "Скорость. Вот и всё. Вот что важно."

**Fix:** Complete sentences. Trust content over presentation.

## Rhetorical Questions Answered Immediately

"The result? Devastating." — Self-posed questions nobody asked, answered for dramatic effect.

**RU:** "Результат? Сокрушительный." / "Что это значит? А вот что..."

**Fix:** Fold into a statement. "The result was devastating." / "Результат оказался провальным."

## Anaphora Abuse

Repeating the same sentence opening 3+ times. One repetition can be effective. Three is a pattern the model can't break.

**RU:** "Они могли бы... Они могли бы... Они могли бы..."

## Tricolon Abuse

Overuse of three-item lists. Use two items or one.

## False Agency

Giving inanimate things human verbs. AI does this to avoid naming the actor.

| Avoid | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| "the complaint becomes a fix" | "the team fixed it" |
| "the decision emerges" | "[who] decided" |
| "the data tells us" | "[who] analyzed the data and found" |

**RU:** "решение появляется" → "[кто] принял решение", "тенденция показывает" → "из данных видно, что..."

## Narrator-from-a-Distance

Floating above the scene instead of putting the reader in it.

| Avoid | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| "Nobody designed this." | "You don't sit down and plan this." |
| "People tend to..." | "You probably..." |

**RU:** "Никто этого не планировал." → "Ты не садишься и не планируешь это."

## Listicle in a Trench Coat

Numbered points disguised as prose: "The first wall... The second wall... The third wall..."

**RU:** "Первая проблема... Вторая проблема... Третья проблема..."

**Fix:** If items don't build on each other, make it an actual list or weave naturally.

## The Signposted Conclusion

"In conclusion..." / "To sum up..." / "Подводя итог..." / "Таким образом..."

**Fix:** Let the conclusion speak for itself. The reader can feel when it's ending.

## "Despite These Challenges..."

AI acknowledges problems only to immediately dismiss them with optimism.

"Despite these challenges, the initiative continues to thrive."

**RU:** "Несмотря на эти трудности, проект продолжает успешно развиваться."

**Fix:** Either the challenges matter (discuss them seriously) or they don't (don't mention them).

## Fractal Summaries

Telling what you'll say → saying it → summarizing what you said. At every level.

**Fix:** Say it once. Trust the reader to remember.

## One-Point Dilution

Making one argument and restating it 10 ways across thousands of words. If you catch the model padding, cut the repetitions.

## Em-Dash Addiction

Humans use 2-3 em dashes per piece. AI uses 20+. Remove them. Use commas or periods.

**RU:** Same applies to overuse of тире (—) as dramatic pause.
