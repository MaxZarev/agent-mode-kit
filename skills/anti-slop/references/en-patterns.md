# English Slop Patterns

## Slop Words

Statistically over-represented in LLM output vs human text. Clusters of these words are a strong AI signal.

### Most flagged single words
delve, tapestry, landscape, crucial, multifaceted, nuanced, pivotal, testament, beacon, embark, foster, realm, endeavor, unleash, intricate, showcasing, underscores, navigate, resonate, paramount, bolster, burgeoning, myriad, plethora, harness, leverage, illuminate, spearhead, juxtaposition, commendable, meticulous, comprehensive, indispensable, unparalleled, transformative, groundbreaking, cutting-edge, game-changer, holistic, synergy, paradigm, ecosystem, stakeholder, streamline, catalyze, galvanize

### Adverbs (kill most of them)
really, just, literally, genuinely, honestly, simply, actually, deeply, truly, fundamentally, inherently, inevitably, interestingly, importantly, crucially, arguably, remarkably, quietly

## Phrases to Remove

### Throat-clearing openers
- "Here's the thing:"
- "Here's what/why/this/that [X]"
- "The uncomfortable truth is"
- "It turns out"
- "The real [X] is"
- "Let me be clear"
- "I'll say it again:"
- "Can we talk about"

### Emphasis crutches
- "Full stop." / "Period."
- "Let that sink in."
- "This matters because"
- "Make no mistake"

### Filler transitions
- "It's worth noting"
- "It bears mentioning"
- "Importantly" / "Interestingly" / "Notably"
- "At its core"
- "In today's [X]"
- "At the end of the day"
- "When it comes to"
- "In a world where"

### Business jargon

| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|-------------|
| Navigate (challenges) | Handle, address |
| Unpack (analysis) | Explain, examine |
| Lean into | Accept, embrace |
| Landscape (context) | Situation, field |
| Game-changer | Significant, important |
| Deep dive | Analysis, examination |
| Moving forward | Next, from now |
| Circle back | Return to, revisit |
| Double down | Commit, increase |

### Meta-commentary
- "In this section, we'll explore..."
- "Let me walk you through..."
- "As we'll see..."
- "I want to explore..."

### Vague declaratives
- "The reasons are structural"
- "The implications are significant"
- "The stakes are high"
- "The consequences are real"

If a sentence says something is important/deep/structural without naming the specific thing, cut it or replace with the specific thing.

## Passive Voice

| Passive | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "X was created" | Name who created it |
| "It is believed that" | Name who believes it |
| "Mistakes were made" | Name who made them |
| "The decision was reached" | Name who decided |
